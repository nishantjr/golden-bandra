Portuguse-Gujerat interactions cover three distinct periods. Initially, when the Portuguese were struggling to find a foothold in the South, the Zamorin in Calicut formed an alliance with the more powerful Gujerat Sultanate which sent contingents, both naval and land based forces, in support.
