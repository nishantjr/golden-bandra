From Bandora to Bandra

Maratha Raj

The Maratha reign was shortlived, from 1739 to 1775, yet it had a major impact on the erstwhile Portuguese territory. They extorted the area ruthlessly, imposing almost a hundred new taxes and cesses, driving the population almost to destitution. One significant consequence was that the Koli community which earlier was subservient to the Kunbis were now more prosperous. While the professed reason for the Maratha invasion was the reestablishment of the hindu dharma, in practice the Marathas were much more benevolent to the Christians and apart from a ghar wapasi campaign to woo the converted population, they allowed them much greater freedom than the Portguuese had allowed the hindus.
